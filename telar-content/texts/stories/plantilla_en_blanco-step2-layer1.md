By the 1960s, Halloween had already become a defining tradition at UCSB and in Isla Vista. Each year, the holiday attracted enormous crowds that transformed Del Playa and the surrounding streets into one of California's largest college party scenes. However, as attendance grew, so did reports of riots, drug abuse, assaults, and arrests. Halloween increasingly developed a reputation not simply as a celebration, but as a dangerous and uncontrollable event. As Isla Vista's image as a party town expanded throughout the late 1980s and early 1990s, local authorities grew more determined to suppress the disorder associated with the holiday.
