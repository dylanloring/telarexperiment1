The Five-Year Plan introduced a 72-hour noise ordinance during Halloween weekend and dramatically expanded police authority throughout Isla Vista. 

Officers adopted a zero-tolerance policy in which even minor violations could result in immediate arrest rather than warnings. 

Police presence intensified through the use of barricades, mounted officers, and large coordinated patrol units designed to quickly shut down gatherings viewed as dangerous or disorderly. 

The operation reportedly cost more than $200,000 annually, demonstrating the extent to which local authorities were willing to invest in preventing another Halloween like 1992.
